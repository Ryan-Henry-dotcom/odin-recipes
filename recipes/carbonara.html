Carbonara -- authentic, creamy, glossy, piping hot -- slides effortlessly down your throat like a cup of warm chocolate ganache. Carbonara.

Spaghetti alla Carbonara Ingredients

  • Oil
  • Cured pork cheek
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Shredded Cheese
  • Seasonings

Method

  1. The essentials: Salt your water; Boil and strain your pasta, making sure NOT to dispose your pasta water (I'm assuming you understand that you need to let the water boil before adding the UNSNAPPED pasta strands); Dice your cured pork.

  2. Cook the cured pork in olive oil until browned and crispy, then drain (and save) the oil.

  3. Whisk in the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some black pepper in a bowl 'til it's smooth

  4. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy. Do that under low heat, otherwise the eggs'll become scrambled.

  5. Stir in the pork, add a tiny amount of that pork oil (and if the pasta isn't as creamy as you'd like it to be, you can stir in small amounts of the pasta water from earlier 'til you're satisfied), then plate it with the remaining cheese and black pepper.
